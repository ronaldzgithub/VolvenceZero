## V10 vs V9: what changed and why

V9 successfully landed the trajectory flywheel and committed parenting as the first wedge. External read said: this is now close to *fundable inevitability*, but two questions remain unanswered, and they are the two that decide whether Patrick can defend the round in his own LP conversation:

**Question 1 — "If foundation labs all ship persistent memory in two years, what do you have left?"** v9 still implicitly answered "deeper memory." That is not defendable; it is exactly the thesis-shape that gets a startup classified as *temporary abstraction* or *memory wrapper*.

**V10 fix 1 — New Slide 7: "Memory is substrate. Relationship governance is the product."** The slide actively concedes that foundation-model memory will get stronger and reframes that as *substrate cost reduction*, freeing the governance layer above. Seven dimensions of relationship governance — what persists / what adapts / what decays / what should never transfer / when monetization stops / how rupture is repaired / what consent means longitudinally — none of them is what a generality-optimizing foundation lab will verticalize. The Q&A is rewritten to make the punchier line: *the moat is not remembering more; the moat is deciding what persists, what adapts, what repairs trust, and what becomes economically actionable over years.*

**Question 2 — "Is parenting venture-scale or just emotionally compelling?"** v9 walked Patrick into a corner where the natural follow-up is *"why not pet care, elder care, wellness, CRM?"* Emotionally intense vertical ≠ venture-scale category.

**V10 fix 2a — Slide 5 reframed.** Parenting is now stated as *the highest-fidelity proof environment*, not the terminal market. Six flywheel conditions are not "why we sell parenting" but "why parenting is the cleanest setting to observe whether relationship-state compounding is real." The new lines that need to land: *Parenting is not the terminal market. It is the cleanest environment for observing whether relationship-state compounding is real.*

**V10 fix 2b — New Slide 12: Phase 1 → 2 → 3 → 4 sequencing.** Phase 1 (parenting, 0-18m): trajectory compounding + longitudinal trust proven. Phase 2 (Mobi B2B2C, 0-12m parallel): commercial lift proven. Phase 3 (enterprise / regulated workflows, 12-36m): governance defensibility proven. Phase 4 (cross-vertical relationship graph, 36m+): platform option. This is the *why-this-wedge-precedes-the-category* slide that converts "parenting company" into "category company starting in parenting."

**V10 fix 3 — New Slide 15: the uncertainty boundary.** Three columns: proven / strongly suspected / long-term belief. Engineering numbers in column 1; flywheel + Mobi conversion in column 2; Generation-2 vertical proprietary data + cross-vertical platform in column 3. **A controlled-humility table is the maturity signal Patrick is hunting for.** Most decks have it implicitly; we put it in the room.

## Slide 7 — Memory is substrate. Relationship governance is the product.

**Claim**

> The most important slide in V10. Foundation labs will ship persistent memory; assume they will be excellent at it. **That helps us — it makes recall a substrate cost, not a product feature, freeing the layer above.** The product is not memory. The product is *relationship governance* — the seven decisions a long-relationship system has to make about its own behavior, none of which a generality-optimizing foundation lab will verticalize.

**On-screen — the framing shift**

> **What foundation-model memory does** (and will do increasingly well):
>
> - Recall what was said
> - Maintain long context
> - Personalize tone
> - Persist preferences
>
> **What foundation-model memory does *not* do** (and will not, because foundation labs optimize for generality across one billion users):
>
> | Governance dimension | The vertical decision a relationship product has to make |
> |---|---|
> | **What persists** | Which states should compound across sessions vs. which should remain in-session only |
> | **What adapts** | Which user signals should change future behavior, on what time-scale |
> | **What decays** | Which states should age out (sleep pattern from 6 months ago) vs. which never decay (formula caution) |

## "Observed" boxes — the operational-realism convention

V10.1 introduces a deliberate texture pattern: **on four high-concept slides (4, 5, 6, 7), a small "Observed" box appears at the bottom of the page.** The convention is:

- The box reports a *single mundane behavioral observation from early closed-alpha pilot* — the kind of thing you could only know if you have actually run the system.
- **No numbers.** "Several parents," "visibly weaker," "in one early interaction pattern" — qualitative, specific, non-marketing.
- **No success claims.** Each observation reports either friction the system encountered, a non-obvious user behavior, or a place where engineering intuition got pushed back by reality.
- The box is *visually small* — never larger than the page punchline above it. It is a footnote that earns the page realism, not a competing claim.
- **Do not consolidate these into a "learnings slide."** The whole point is that they are dropped *into* high-concept pages so those pages stop reading as theory.
