## V9 vs V8: what changed and why

V8 successfully shifted from manifesto to investment document — narrative density down, business language up, Mobi as concrete commercial test. External read on v8 was sharp: still close to "top seed deck" but not yet "fundable inevitability." Three diagnoses and three fixes:

**Diagnosis 1 — "Platform vs application company" ambiguity.** v8 alternated between runtime / state architecture / governance substrate language and parenting / Mobi / companion / commerce wedges. A VC reading v8 cannot say what the first wedge is.

**Fix 1 — Commit to parenting as THE first wedge.** Slides 5-6 are now parenting-only. Mobi becomes the *second* wedge and the proof that the same engine produces measurable conversion lift in B2B2C — but it is not the lede. The other four verticals appear once on Slide 10 as the revenue map, not as parallel bets.

**Diagnosis 2 — Missing flywheel.** v8 said relationship continuity creates economic lift, but never showed *why it compounds and locks in.* Without a flywheel, VC stays in "interesting thesis" mode rather than "this gets better as it ages, and switching cost rises non-linearly."

**Fix 2 — New Slide 4: The Trajectory Flywheel.** Eight stages, each with (a) the metric we measure, (b) the economic consequence, (c) the irreversibility property. The flywheel pivots on a single non-obvious insight: *in parenting, the child is the third party — and the child changes faster than any system without 18 months of family-specific observation can keep up.* Switching cost is not "user has to re-onboard"; it is "user loses 18 months of irreplaceable developmental history."

**Diagnosis 3 — Architecture prose density.** v8 mentions "runtime / state / identity / governance / persistent / continuity" too many times in the body; cognitive fatigue hits by Slide 8.

**Fix 3 — Trim ~30% of body architecture prose.** The architecture argument now lives mostly in Slide 9 (governance moat) and Appendix E (technical DD). Slides 5-7 carry the argument through a concrete 12-week parenting trajectory — *show* not *describe*.

What was kept from v8:

- Generation-2 vertical proprietary data spine (Slide 3) — this remains the deck's anchor against Xfund's own portfolio thesis.
- Mobi unit economics, kill criterion, RMB 5.6M / 16.8M / 33.6M ramp (now Slide 8, compressed from three slides to one).
- Governance moat for Patrick's JD-MBA background (Slide 9, trimmed).
- Yang Liu math + engineering credibility (Slide 12).
- "Frontier labs optimize for generality; we optimize for relationship state" framing — replacing all "OpenAI cannot do X" language.
- Honest disclosure of what is not yet proven, including thesis-level kill criterion.
- Appendix E preserves v6/v7/v8 technical chain for senior technical DD partner.
