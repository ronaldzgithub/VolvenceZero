## Slide 7 — Memory is substrate. Governance is the product. (4 min) — MUST LAND

If foundation labs ship persistent memory in two years — excellently — what's left? Not "deeper memory." **Memory is substrate; relationship governance is the product.**

Foundation memory does recall, long context, tone, preferences. *That helps us — makes that layer cheaper.*

Seven decisions it does *not* solve: what persists vs. in-session; what adapts on what time scale; what decays (6-month sleep pattern ages out; formula caution doesn't); what never transfers + audit evidence; when monetization stops after rupture; how rupture repairs into behavioral change; what consent means longitudinally.

**A foundation lab optimizes for one billion users; relationship governance is per-vertical, per-relationship, per-jurisdiction. They will not verticalize it.**

**The moat is not remembering more. The moat is deciding what persists, what adapts, what repairs trust, and what becomes economically actionable over years.**

Observed: system over-intervened on a parent who'd put the phone down. **Re-engagement only came after the agent acknowledged its own uncertainty.** *Repair, not avoidance, was the operative primitive.*
