Problem:
Today's AI systems are already extremely powerful, yet they still have two fundamental flaws. First, they rely mainly on offline pre-training, updated only once every six months or longer, unable to truly learn in the moment. Second, from goal setting, environment perception, and action selection to feedback correction, this intelligence loop is still mainly completed by humans. As a result, it is more like a powerful tool than a truly experienced agent.

Positioning:
If we want to achieve true intelligence, the core is not to build yet another larger model, but to give the system the ability to learn continuously online. It must be able to form abstractions, transfer experience, and continually correct its own behavior from a small amount of feedback in sparse, incomplete, high-noise real environments. There are two key challenges here: sparse data and abstraction ability.

Technical Foundation:
These are precisely the two core capability lines our team has accumulated over the past fifteen years, both underpinned by mathematical guarantees and empirical backing—not thrown together on the fly:

- **Addressing sparse data → Active Learning.** Under the real constraints of non-stationary, streaming users with extremely few annotations (only hundreds of interactions per month), the mathematical guarantees of active learning turn "efficient learning from little feedback" from a vision into a provable capability.

- **Addressing abstraction ability → Experience Induction + Transfer Learning.** On one hand, we let the system form reusable structures from the experience stream rather than memorizing surface text; on the other hand, transfer learning mathematically guarantees that these structures and experiences can be reused across scenarios and across users. This direction also has the cleanest empirical backing at the level of human brain perception.

These two capability lines are ultimately engineered into a single self-developed runtime and delivered stably, reusable at scale across vertical life-forms—moving from the positioning of "forming abstractions, transferring experience, and continuously self-correcting in sparse, high-noise real environments" to a real system that can be delivered at scale.

Finally, the vision:

Rich Sutton says machines need to truly master goals and experience. That is exactly what Volvence sets out to do: let AI not merely call upon knowledge, but form goals, experience, feedback, and self-correction at the cognitive level. Only when machines possess this kind of continuous learning and cognitive closed loop can digital life, emotional intelligence, human-machine relationships, and true artificial general intelligence become possible.

Business Model:

We turn this set of cognitive capabilities into Digital Life as a Service (DLaaS)—making cognition a system that is **transferable, evolvable, and able to serve the world**.

Transferable means the same cognitive kernel can be reused across scenarios, industries, and users, rather than rebuilt from scratch for every customer. Evolvable means it continuously learns, accumulates experience, and self-corrects through long-range interaction with real users—the more it is used, the better it understands and the more valuable it becomes. Able to serve the world means it is delivered externally through a standardized runtime and contract interfaces, so that any product, brand, or enterprise can plug into a digital life-form that offers long-term companionship and continuous growth. We have already established 3 joint-venture companies, covering a base of 45 million fans and 20,000 enterprise customers, forming the real foundation for scalable replication of DLaaS.
